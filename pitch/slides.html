What's the difference between a 401 and a 403 response?
A 401 means "who are you?" (unauthenticated), while a 403 means "I know who you are, but you can't do that" (unauthorized).
So if a user's JWT is expired, is that a 401 or 403?
That's a 401. The user was authenticated but their proof of identity (the JWT) is no longer valid. They need to re-authenticate.
What about if they have a valid token but try to access an admin endpoint?
That's a classic 403 Forbidden. Their identity is confirmed (valid token) but their role doesn't have permission for that resource.
