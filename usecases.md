## Use Cases

### — AAA+ —

### 1. Negotiation Blind Spots [AAA+, intel-layer?]
You're emailing about salary, rent, a freelance rate, a car price:
- "You're underselling yourself — the market rate for this is 20-30% higher"
- "They said 'final offer' but they've been responding within minutes — they're interested"
- "You're about to agree to their first number. You almost always have room to counter"
- "This landlord listed the same unit for $200 less 3 months ago"

The confidence boost with actual data behind it.

### 2. First Impression Prep [AAA+]
You have a meeting, date, or event with someone new:
- "Quick brief: she's VP of Marketing at [company], 2 years there, previously at [other company]. You have 3 mutual connections"
- "He posted about [topic] last week — easy conversation starter"
- "Last time you met with this team, the open action item was [X] — it's still not done"
- "You're meeting them in 20 minutes and you haven't looked them up yet — here's what you need to know"

You walk into every room prepared without doing any prep.

### 3. The Critic [AAA+, intel-layer, bullshit-detector]
Your second-opinion guy. Knows more than you, knows the real cost, checks the bullshit. Activates whenever you're about to spend money, sign something, or trust a claim.

**Real estate:**
- "This has been on the market 90 days — unusual for this neighborhood. Probably overpriced"
- "The photos don't show the kitchen — that's usually intentional"
- "The HOA fee went up 40% in 2 years — red flag for the reserve fund"
- "Street view shows a construction site next door that isn't in any of the photos"

**Cars:**
- "This is $3k above KBB value for the mileage"
- "This model year had a recall for transmission issues — check if it was serviced"
- "3 owners in 4 years — red flag"
- "Same car, fewer miles, $2k less at a dealer 10 miles from here"

**Products & labels:**
- "This says 'natural' but the third ingredient is a synthetic preservative"
- "'Sugar-free' but it has 4 types of artificial sweetener"
- "The store brand is literally the same manufacturer for half the price"
- "The serving size is deliberately small to make the calories look low — you'd actually eat 3x this"

**Warranties & returns:**
- "You bought this laptop 11 months ago — manufacturer warranty expires next month. If anything's off, now's the time"
- "That shirt you ordered? Return window closes in 3 days and you haven't worn it"
- "The 30-day price match window on that TV is still open and it just dropped $60"
- "Your AppleCare expires in 2 weeks — that cracked corner you've been ignoring?"

The friend who's annoyingly good at catching what you'd miss — and saves you thousands over time.

### — AAA —

### 4. Relationship Keeper [AAA]
You're not a bad friend, you're just busy:
- "You haven't talked to [close friend] in 6 weeks — that's the longest gap in a year"
- "Your friend just got a new job (you saw the LinkedIn post) — you haven't congratulated them"
- "Mom's birthday is in 3 days and you haven't gotten anything"
- "You and [person] used to talk every week — it's been 2 months. Just a 'hey' goes a long way"

Not a CRM. Just the part of your brain that tracks people you care about but life gets in the way.

### 5. Fact Check in Real Time [AAA, intel-layer]
You're reading a thread, an article, someone's claims:
- "That stat they're quoting is from 2019 and was debunked the same year"
- "This article cites a study — the study actually says the opposite of what they're claiming"
- "You're about to reply to this person — they post bait like this daily for engagement. Don't feed it"
- "This 'breaking news' is 3 months old being recirculated"

The bullshit filter for everything you consume online.

### 6. Impulse Cooldown [AAA]
Not judging, just a pause:
- "You've added 4 things to cart in 10 minutes — this is impulse territory"
- "You searched for this right after seeing an ad. The ad used urgency tactics — there's no real deadline"
- "You bought something similar 6 months ago and used it twice"
- "You've been browsing for an hour and your cart is at $340 — want to sleep on it?"

The 3-second pause between wanting and buying.

### 7. Recipe Sidekick [AAA]
You're looking at a recipe. The overlay:
- Auto-converts measurements (cups to grams, F to C)
- "This takes 2 hours including prep — you probably want to start the marinade now"
- "You could substitute Greek yogurt for sour cream here"
- Builds a clean grocery list from the ingredients on screen

### 8. Tab Comparison [AAA]
You have two tabs open — two apartments, two laptops, two flights. The overlay notices and:
- Generates a side-by-side comparison table
- Highlights the key differences
- "The second one is better value but has worse reviews for noise"

### 9. Movie / Show Matchmaker [AAA]
You open Netflix, or IMDB, or just google "good movies." Before you type anything:
- "Based on what you've watched recently: you'd probably love [X]"
- "That one you almost watched last week? People who liked [thing you liked] rated it 9/10"
- Knows your taste — doesn't suggest horror if you always skip it

### 10. Research Synthesizer [AAA, short-history-dependent, heavy-reasoning]
You're reading multiple articles or papers on the same topic. The overlay:
- "You've read 4 articles about [topic]. Here's what they agree on, and where they disagree"
- "This article contradicts what the first one said about X"
- Builds a running summary as you browse

### 11. Social Context [AAA, social-apps-context]
You're about to message someone, or looking at their profile:
- "You last talked to them 3 weeks ago about [topic]"
- "They posted about [thing] recently — could be a good opener"
- Knows your social patterns without being creepy — just helpful memory

### 12. Food Ordering Memory [AAA, history-dependent]
You open UberEats / DoorDash. Before you even scroll:
- "Last time you got pad thai from that place you didn't finish it. This place has better ratings for Thai"
- "You always order the same thing from here — want to try their new menu item? It's similar to what you like"
- "It's Tuesday, you usually do something light — here's a salad spot you haven't tried"

Knows your food patterns, your go-tos, and when you're in a rut.

### 13. Energy Matching [AAA, great UI idea]
The overlay reads the vibe of your whole session and adapts:
- Morning: bright, warm tones. Suggestions are energizing
- Post-lunch: notices the slump, glow gets stimulating, maybe surfaces something interesting to re-engage you
- Late night deep work: goes almost invisible, dark mode within dark mode, just stays out of the way
- Weekend: completely different personality. Looser, more fun suggestions

The UI itself shifts with your energy, not just the content.

### 14. Gift Radar [AAA, can be good if you browse convo + then search gifts, this is smart]
You're browsing someone's Instagram or their wishlist or just talking about them:
- "Based on what they post about, they'd probably love [X]"
- "Their birthday is in 2 weeks" (if it's seen calendar or social info)
- "Budget-friendly option: [Y]. Premium option: [Z]"

Solves the "I never know what to get people" problem.

### 15. Design Second Opinion [AAA, intel-layer, short-history-dependent]
You're making a slide deck, picking colors for a project, or laying out a poster:
- "The contrast on that text is hard to read — try #F5F5F5 on that background"
- "Your last 3 slides all look the same — maybe break it up with a full-bleed image here"
- "That font pairing is fighting itself — these two work better together"
- You're choosing between two logo options: "The left one reads better at small sizes"

Like having a designer friend looking over your shoulder.

### 16. Group Chat Catch-Up [AAA]
You open a group chat with 200+ unread messages:
- "Here's what you missed: they decided on Saturday for dinner, Jake can't come, and there's a debate about where to go"
- "Someone asked you a direct question 45 messages up — [shows it]"
- "The vibe shifted around message 80 — someone brought up politics, it got weird, then they moved on"

Saves you from the scroll of doom.

### 17. Fine Print Scanner / Privacy agreement checker [AAA, intel-layer, read-privacy for me]
You're clicking "I agree" without reading. Everyone does. The overlay actually reads it:
- "This auto-renews at $49/month after the trial"
- "They can sell your data to third parties — that's buried in section 12"
- "There's no cancellation option after 14 days — you're locked in for a year"
- "This is actually a good deal, the terms are clean"

Every terms of service, every subscription checkbox, every "continue" button — it's reading what you won't.

### 18. Scam & Phishing Shield [AAA, intel-layer]
Your parents need this. You need this. Everyone does:
- "This email says it's from Apple but the domain is app1e-support.com"
- "This site looks like your bank but the URL is off by one character"
- "This 'job offer' is a template used in known scams"
- "That DM with the 'investment opportunity'? The account was created 4 days ago"

Catches what your eye skims over.

### 19. The Number Doesn't Match [AAA, intel-layer]
You're reading a report, a listing, a pitch, someone's claims:
- "They say revenue grew 40% but the graph shows 28%"
- "This apartment says 800 sqft but based on the floor plan it's closer to 600"
- "The listing says 'recently renovated' but the photos show old fixtures"
- "This resume says they led a team of 50 but their LinkedIn shows they were there 4 months"

Catches inconsistencies humans gloss over because we trust what's written.

### 20. Health Doom Spiral Interrupter [AAA, intel-layer, reassurance-health-help]
You're googling symptoms at 2am. Of course you are:
- "The most likely explanation is [simple thing]. This is almost never [scary thing]"
- "You've been on WebMD for 20 minutes and your searches are escalating — take a breath"
- "If you actually have [specific symptoms together], see a doctor. Otherwise this is probably nothing"
- "You googled this same thing 3 months ago and you were fine"

Stops the spiral. Gives you the honest take.

### 21. Review Behind the Review [AAA, intel-layer]
You're reading reviews for a product, restaurant, or service:
- "80% of the 5-star reviews were posted within 2 days — likely incentivized"
- "The 1-star reviews all mention the same thing: customer service. The product itself is rated well"
- "This place has 4.8 stars but only 12 reviews. The one next door has 4.4 with 800 reviews — probably the safer bet"
- "The top review is from someone who reviewed 200 products this month"

Reads reviews like a skeptic so you don't have to.

### 22. Forgot Something [AAA, history-dependent]
A catch-all for the obvious things you miss when you're in motion:
- You're booking a trip: "You haven't looked at visa requirements — this country needs one for your passport"
- You're RSVPing to a dinner: "That's the same night you said you'd help your friend move"
- You're buying a gift: "You got them the same thing last year"
- You're about to join a video call: "You have food on your face" (vision model catches it)

Your screenshots become an actual memory, not a graveyard.

- You're heated: "You might want to wait 10 minutes before sending this"
- "You said 'no worries!' but the rest of the message sounds like there are worries"

Not writing for you. Just a gut check.
